I am giving up and moving on :D

Dec 11, 2015 by Mahta

I have iTune on my MacBook. I have many music in the library and it’s relatively easy to deal with them in a 13” display and different user interface that I experience on MacBook. In past (when I first started using iTune on my iPhone) I was able to selectively add songs to my phone and listen to them whenever. Sure I needed to manually move the songs to my phone but on the bright side I only had to deal with few albums and songs that I really wanted to have with me everywhere as opposed to my entire massive library. But the new iTune app just added everything to my phone library automatically, and given the weak user interface/ user experience of iTune app in iPhone (and in general), browsing for what I want is becoming my nightmare. I’m not using the app on my phone any more :(.

Don't you find this confusing? :S

Nov 29, 2015 by Mahta

One of the website that I use a lot is Ticketmaster. If you've ever used this website you might know that you can add your favorite acts in your list of favorites so when what you like is happening near you, you'll be notified about it via email.

What is confusing for me is the button that they use for this matter. To be more specific it's the color of the button. So before you add your favorite let say artist, the button looks like what you see in the top picture. It's blue and it is explicitly telling you to add this artist to your list, but that is not the confusing part. After you do that the message changes to my favorite and the color turns to gray.

Now what is confusing to me is, let say one month later when I am checking some tickets and stuff and I see that gray button beside something that I like I click on it because the gray color is telling me that this function is not active (the artist is not in my list of favorites) and the moment I click on it I get the blue button and I realize, oh ya it was already in my list. I think it would be way easier if the blue button was gray and once you click on it, it would change to red, demonstrating deactive and active. Well maybe that’s just me!
