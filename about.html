About me :D

Whenever I think about UX design the first thing that comes to my mind is “Don’t make me think!”, because not only it’s the name of my favorite book about human-computer interaction and web usability (by Steve Krug), but also it is the main goal of the design. In my mind, there is no better way to summarize all the strategies and steps needed for a good design in one sentence.

As a UX designer my challenge is to deliver the most elegant and easy grasp design, so the user can intuitively navigate and find anything that we are offering and they are there to see. To accomplish this goal I have to take different steps:

  • Communicate effectively with client at all times.
  • Writing the story (define the problem in hand), research, and then discuss possible solutions.
  • Use wireframe and prototyping to bring those solutions to life.
  • Using scenarios and personas to criticize the potential solutions and make the changes based on the feedback of the team.
  • Get more feedback from real user through usability testing and take them to account.

Sometimes it might be a good idea to go through some of these steps more than once to get it right!

I keep in mind that, as a designer it can be harder to see the flaws of my own design, because I know too much about it and I am too familiar with it (that is why usability testing is a very important part of the cycle). Therefore a very important aspect of being a good designer is begin a good observer and be open to others ideas and criticism.
